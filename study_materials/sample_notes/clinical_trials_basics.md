# Clinical Trials: Key Concepts

## What is a Clinical Trial?
A clinical trial is a research study that tests how well a new medical approach
works in people. This includes new drugs, devices, diagnostic tests, and
prevention approaches.

## Trial Phases
- **Phase I**: Safety testing. Small group (20-80 people). Is the drug safe?
- **Phase II**: Efficacy testing. Larger group (100-300). Does it work?
- **Phase III**: Comparative testing. Large group (1000-3000). Better than existing?
- **Phase IV**: Post-market surveillance. Long-term effects after approval.

## Key Roles
- **Principal Investigator (PI)**: The lead researcher responsible for the trial
- **Clinical Trial Assistant (CTA)**: Coordinates day-to-day trial operations
- **Clinical Research Associate (CRA)**: Monitors trial sites for compliance
- **IRB (Institutional Review Board)**: Ethics committee that approves and oversees trials

## Clinical Trial Assistant Responsibilities
- Maintaining Trial Master File (TMF), the official regulatory document archive
- Coordinating meetings between site staff, sponsors, and CROs
- Managing investigator site files
- Tracking regulatory submissions and approvals
- Supporting protocol deviations documentation
- Data entry and query resolution
- Managing patient recruitment tracking

## Key Documents
- **Protocol**: The detailed plan of the trial (objectives, design, methodology)
- **ICF (Informed Consent Form)**: Patient consent documentation
- **IND (Investigational New Drug application)**: FDA approval to begin trials
- **CRF (Case Report Form)**: Structured forms for collecting patient data
- **TMF (Trial Master File)**: Complete regulatory archive of the trial
- **SAE (Serious Adverse Event)**: Unexpected harmful events requiring immediate reporting

## Regulatory Framework
- **ICH GCP (Good Clinical Practice)**: International ethical and scientific standards
- **FDA 21 CFR Part 11**: Electronic records and signatures regulations
- **GDPR/HIPAA**: Patient data privacy regulations

## Common Acronyms
CTA, CRA, PI, IRB, TMF, ICF, IND, CRF, SAE, AE, GCP, SOP, EDC, DMC, DSMB
